4. Sharing Intelligence

If the intelligence report contains information that may assist a foreign law enforcement agency, then you should send it to your national intelligence unit.

 

The forwarding agency can search for and enter information into international databases and issue notices and alerts.

The intelligence unit should consider sending such intelligence through foreign liaison officers, or international or regional mechanisms (BLO).

Field officers should complete an intelligence report and submit it to national or regional headquarters who will disseminate it to the appropriate agency.
